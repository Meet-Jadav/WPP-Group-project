Rewind through 2012
NASA's Curiosity Rover Lands on Mars
On August 6, 2012, NASA’s Curiosity rover successfully landed on the Martian surface inside Gale Crater, beginning its historic mission to explore the Red Planet.

Curiosity provided incredible insights into Mars’ geology and climate, and found signs of ancient water flow — a key step in the search for life beyond Earth.
Mary Kom Makes Olympic History
On August 8, 2012, Indian boxer Mary Kom won a bronze medal in the women’s flyweight category at the London Olympics, becoming the first Indian woman boxer to do so.

Her achievement was a breakthrough moment for women in Indian sports and inspired a generation of athletes across the country.
Yash Chopra’s Final Film *Jab Tak Hai Jaan* Released
On November 13, 2012, the legendary director Yash Chopra’s final film, *Jab Tak Hai Jaan*, starring Shah Rukh Khan, Katrina Kaif, and Anushka Sharma, was released.

The film was a huge success and is remembered for its emotional portrayal of love and loss. Yash Chopra’s death shortly before the film’s release made it even more iconic.
India Erupts in Protests Over Delhi Gang Rape
On December 16, 2012, a brutal gang rape and assault of a 23-year-old woman in Delhi led to massive public outcry and protests demanding justice and stronger laws.

The case, later known as the Nirbhaya case, led to nationwide debates and significant legal reforms to protect women’s rights in India.
India's Best-Ever Olympic Performance (Till Then)
On August 12, 2012, as the London Olympics came to a close, India celebrated its best Olympic performance to date, winning a total of six medals — two silver and four bronze.

Medalists included Sushil Kumar (silver, wrestling), Vijay Kumar (silver, shooting), Mary Kom (bronze, boxing), Gagan Narang (bronze, shooting), Yogeshwar Dutt (bronze, wrestling), and Saina Nehwal (bronze, badminton). The Games were a major milestone for Indian sports.
Apple Unveils the iPhone 5
On September 12, 2012, Apple launched the iPhone 5 with a larger screen and new features, which revolutionized the smartphone industry.

The iPhone 5 was thinner, lighter, and introduced the Lightning connector, setting the stage for future innovations in Apple's smartphone lineup.
