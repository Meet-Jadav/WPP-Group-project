Satyam Scandal Unfolds
On January 7, 2009, Satyam’s chairman Ramalinga Raju admitted to a ₹7,000 crore accounting fraud, shaking investor confidence across India.

The scandal was one of the largest corporate frauds in India, leading to stricter corporate governance laws and government intervention to rescue the company.
Chandrayaan-1 Detects Water on the Moon
On February 20, 2009, ISRO’s lunar mission discovered water molecules on the Moon’s surface — a breakthrough in space exploration.

Although Chandrayaan-1 lost contact earlier, data from its instruments confirmed water presence, later validated by NASA. It was a historic scientific achievement for India.
General Elections 2009 Results
On May 16, 2009, UPA retained power in India’s Lok Sabha elections, with Dr. Manmohan Singh returning as Prime Minister.

The elections were held in five phases. The Congress-led alliance secured a stable majority, and Dr. Singh became the first PM to return after completing a full 5-year term since Nehru.
Section 377 Decriminalized
On July 2, 2009, the Delhi High Court read down Section 377, decriminalizing consensual same-sex relationships.

This judgment was a major victory for LGBTQ+ rights in India. Though later challenged, it paved the way for the 2018 Supreme Court decision affirming the ruling nationwide.
India Secures Champions Trophy Qualification
On September 28, 2009, India qualified for the next ICC Champions Trophy due to solid international performance.

With Dhoni as captain, India had a dominant year in cricket, preparing to win the Champions Trophy in 2013, continuing its global success in all formats.
Microsoft Launches Windows 7
On October 22, 2009, Microsoft officially released Windows 7, succeeding the unpopular Windows Vista.

Windows 7 brought performance improvements, a clean interface, and better user experience, quickly becoming one of the most popular operating systems globally.
US House Passes Healthcare Reform Bill
On November 7, 2009, the U.S. House of Representatives passed a key healthcare reform bill under President Obama’s leadership.

This was a major step toward what would become the Affordable Care Act (ACA) in 2010, expanding healthcare access for millions of Americans.
Tendulkar Nears Test Century Milestone
On December 15, 2009, Sachin Tendulkar came closer to achieving his 50th Test century, thrilling fans worldwide.

That year, he also became the first cricketer to cross 17,000 ODI runs, reinforcing his legendary status in international cricket.

Year 2009: A mix of major milestones in science, rights, politics, technology, sports, and the environment.
