Rewind through 2018
Statue of Unity Inaugurated
On October 31, 2018, India unveiled the Statue of Unity in Gujarat, honoring Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.

At 182 meters tall, it became the tallest statue in the world. The monument celebrates Patel’s role in unifying India after independence. Built on the banks of the Narmada River, it quickly became a major tourist destination and a symbol of national pride. Its construction also boosted local development and infrastructure.
Kerala Floods: One of the Worst in a Century
In August 2018, Kerala was devastated by massive floods due to unusually heavy monsoon rains.

Over 400 lives were lost and thousands were displaced as water submerged towns and villages. It caused widespread destruction of homes, roads, and farmlands. The floods triggered one of the largest rescue and relief operations in India. It highlighted the urgent need for better environmental and disaster preparedness strategies.
India’s First Engine-Less Train – Train 18 Launched
In November 2018, India launched Train 18, its first engine-less semi-high-speed train, later named Vande Bharat Express.

The train was developed indigenously under the “Make in India” initiative. It featured modern facilities, smart automation, and a top speed of 180 km/h. Train 18 marked a leap forward in Indian rail travel, representing efficiency, comfort, and futuristic design for domestic routes.
Supreme Court Allows Women into Sabarimala Temple
On September 28, 2018, the Supreme Court ruled that women of all ages could enter Kerala’s Sabarimala temple.

This decision ended the traditional ban on women aged 10–50 years from entering the temple. The verdict sparked widespread debates, protests, and legal challenges. While many saw it as a triumph for gender equality, others viewed it as interference in religious customs. The issue remained a flashpoint in social discourse.
India Launches National Healthcare Scheme – Ayushman Bharat
On September 23, 2018, Ayushman Bharat, the world’s largest health insurance scheme, was launched by Prime Minister Modi.

It aimed to provide free healthcare up to ₹5 lakh per year to over 50 crore economically vulnerable citizens. The scheme targeted secondary and tertiary healthcare needs across public and private hospitals. Ayushman Bharat was a landmark step toward universal health coverage in India.
Supreme Court Decriminalizes Section 377
On September 6, 2018, the Supreme Court struck down parts of Section 377, decriminalizing same-sex relations.

The decision was a monumental victory for the LGBTQ+ community in India. It ended the criminalization of private, consensual relationships between adults and upheld the values of dignity, freedom, and equality. The ruling was widely praised and considered a transformative moment in Indian civil rights history.
Launch of India’s Heaviest Satellite – GSAT-11
On December 5, 2018, ISRO successfully launched GSAT-11, India’s heaviest satellite, from French Guiana.

Weighing nearly 5,854 kg, GSAT-11 aimed to enhance high-speed internet access across India, especially in remote areas. It was a key part of India's broader Digital India initiative. The satellite's success demonstrated India’s growing space capabilities and ISRO’s commitment to next-gen communication infrastructure.
