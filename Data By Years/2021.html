COVID-19 Second Wave in India
In April-May 2021, India faced a devastating second wave of COVID-19.

Hospitals ran out of oxygen and beds as daily cases crossed 4 lakhs. The Delta variant was a major cause.
Vaccination Drive Begins
On January 16, 2021, India launched the world’s largest COVID-19 vaccination campaign.

Vaccines like Covishield and Covaxin were rolled out in phases, starting with healthcare workers.
Tokyo Olympics: Neeraj Chopra’s Gold
On August 7, 2021, Neeraj Chopra won India’s first-ever Olympic gold in athletics (javelin throw).

His throw of 87.58 meters brought India its second individual Olympic gold in history.
Cyclone Tauktae and Cyclone Yaas
In May 2021, back-to-back cyclones struck India's west and east coasts.

Tauktae affected Gujarat and Maharashtra, while Yaas hit Odisha and West Bengal, causing large-scale evacuations.
Farm Laws Repealed
On November 19, 2021, Prime Minister Modi announced the repeal of the three controversial farm laws.

This came after year-long farmer protests, especially from Punjab, Haryana, and Uttar Pradesh.
India Crosses 100 Crore Vaccine Doses
On October 21, 2021, India administered its 1 billionth COVID-19 vaccine dose.

This milestone marked a significant achievement in India's fight against the pandemic.

Year 2021: A year of resilience and record-breaking milestones for India.
