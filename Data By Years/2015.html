Launch of Windows 10
On July 29, 2015, Microsoft launched Windows 10, a major update to its operating system with new features and improvements.

Windows 10 brought several improvements, including the return of the Start Menu and the introduction of the Edge browser. It was designed for a seamless experience across PCs, tablets, and smartphones. Microsoft offered Windows 10 as a free upgrade to users of Windows 7 and 8, significantly boosting its adoption. The new system was well-received for its enhanced performance, security, and user interface, quickly becoming one of the most popular operating systems worldwide.
ISRO Launches Astrosat
India’s first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory, Astrosat, was launched in 2015 to study the universe in various wavelengths.

Launched on September 28, 2015, Astrosat was a significant milestone for ISRO in space exploration. It aimed to observe the universe in ultraviolet, visible, and X-ray wavelengths. The mission placed India among the few nations capable of deploying space-based observatories. Astrosat provided valuable data that advanced scientific understanding of stars, galaxies, and other cosmic phenomena, strengthening India’s position in global space research.
Nepal Earthquake
On April 25, 2015, a devastating earthquake struck Nepal, causing widespread destruction and loss of life.

This 7.8-magnitude earthquake resulted in over 9,000 fatalities and massive damage to infrastructure, especially in Kathmandu and surrounding areas. Iconic landmarks were destroyed, and avalanches, including one on Mount Everest, trapped climbers. The international community responded with aid and relief efforts, offering support to rebuild the nation and assist the survivors in the aftermath of this tragic disaster.
India Eliminated Maternal and Neonatal Tetanus
India was officially declared free from maternal and neonatal tetanus by the World Health Organization in 2015.

India’s achievement of eliminating maternal and neonatal tetanus was a major victory in public health. The government’s vaccination initiatives, particularly in rural areas, led to the significant reduction of this disease, which had caused many maternal and infant deaths. This success marked a turning point in India’s ongoing efforts to improve healthcare and safeguard vulnerable populations, especially mothers and newborns.
Rise of 4G & Mobile Wallets in India
The rapid growth of 4G networks and the rise of mobile wallets revolutionized digital payments in India in 2015.

2015 saw the widespread adoption of 4G networks, making high-speed internet more accessible across India. Alongside this, mobile wallets like Paytm grew in popularity, enabling people to make digital payments easily, especially in rural areas. This shift helped reduce reliance on cash and encouraged the use of mobile technology for financial transactions, contributing to India’s push toward a cashless economy.
Baahubali: The Beginning Breaks Box Office Records
The release of *Baahubali: The Beginning* in 2015 became a major cinematic milestone, setting box office records.

The film became the highest-grossing of the year in India and was noted for its groundbreaking visual effects, epic storytelling, and action sequences. Directed by S.S. Rajamouli, *Baahubali* was praised for elevating Indian cinema to global standards. Its success set the stage for the sequel, *Baahubali: The Conclusion*, which continued its legacy as one of the most successful film franchises in Indian cinema.
India Overtakes the U.S. in Number of Internet Users
In 2015, India surpassed the U.S. to become the second-largest internet user base globally.

By the end of 2015, India’s internet user base grew rapidly, driven by affordable smartphones and data plans. This growth led to India overtaking the U.S. in terms of internet users, making it the second-largest market globally. The increased connectivity fueled growth in e-commerce, entertainment, and online services, contributing to India’s digital revolution and fostering greater inclusivity.
Google Launches Free WiFi in Indian Railway Stations
Google partnered with Railtel to bring free WiFi to major Indian railway stations, enhancing digital access for millions.

In 2015, Google introduced free WiFi services at select Indian railway stations in partnership with Railtel. This initiative provided high-speed internet access to millions of passengers, especially in rural areas where internet connectivity was limited. The project played a key role in bridging the digital divide, offering an opportunity for millions to connect to the internet and access online services during their travels.
