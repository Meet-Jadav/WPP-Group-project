Rewind through 2004
Indian Ocean Tsunami
On December 26, 2004, a massive earthquake in the Indian Ocean triggered a devastating tsunami.

Over 10,000 people lost their lives in coastal regions of India, especially in Tamil Nadu and the Andaman & Nicobar Islands.
Lok Sabha Elections 2004
Held in four phases between April 20 and May 10, 2004, the elections saw a surprising result.

The Congress-led UPA defeated the NDA government, and Dr. Manmohan Singh became Prime Minister on May 22.
Chandrayaan-1 Preparation Begins
In 2004, ISRO ramped up work on its first lunar mission, Chandrayaan-1.

This mission, later launched in 2008, marked India’s entry into deep space exploration planning during this year.
Smart City Plans in Hyderabad
In early 2004, Hyderabad initiated major tech and infrastructure plans.

Projects like HITEC City expansion positioned it as a future IT and smart city hub.
Rural Electrification Initiative
On August 15, 2004, India announced a major push for rural electrification.

The Rajiv Gandhi Grameen Vidyutikaran Yojana aimed to provide electricity to over 1 lakh villages.
Mangalore Special Economic Zone (SEZ)
On September 24, 2004, the Mangalore SEZ was officially approved.

It was designed to attract foreign investment and boost industrial development in Karnataka.
