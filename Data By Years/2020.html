India's Nationwide COVID-19 Lockdown
On March 24, 2020, Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced a 21-day lockdown to curb COVID-19.

It was the world’s largest lockdown, affecting over 1.3 billion citizens and extended in multiple phases.
Migrant Workers' Crisis
During the COVID-19 lockdown, millions of migrant workers walked hundreds of kilometers to reach their hometowns.

Lack of transport, food, and shelter led to one of India’s largest humanitarian crises in recent times.
India-China Galwan Valley Clash
On June 15, 2020, Indian and Chinese troops clashed in Eastern Ladakh’s Galwan Valley.

20 Indian soldiers were martyred, marking the first deadly India-China border conflict since 1975.
Cyclone Amphan Hits Eastern India
On May 20, 2020, Cyclone Amphan made landfall in West Bengal and Odisha.

It was one of the strongest cyclones in the Bay of Bengal in decades, causing massive destruction and displacing millions.
Ram Mandir Bhoomi Pujan
On August 5, 2020, Prime Minister Modi laid the foundation stone for the Ram Temple in Ayodhya.

The ceremony was held after the Supreme Court’s 2019 verdict, resolving a decades-long dispute.
Launch of Atmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyan
In May 2020, PM Modi launched the Atmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyan to promote self-reliance in India’s economy.

The package was worth ₹20 lakh crore and focused on local manufacturing, MSMEs, and startups.
