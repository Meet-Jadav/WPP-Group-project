Demonetization in India
On November 8, 2016, Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced the demonetization of ₹500 and ₹1000 notes.

The move aimed to curb black money, counterfeit currency, and corruption. It led to immediate cash shortages, long ATM queues, and widespread public inconvenience. While digital payments surged and the formal economy expanded, the policy sparked intense debate about its long-term effectiveness. It remains one of the boldest financial reforms in India’s recent history.
Launch of Reliance Jio
Reliance Jio launched commercially in September 2016, offering free calls and low-cost high-speed data.

Jio’s entry revolutionized the telecom sector, making mobile internet accessible to millions. It triggered a price war, forced competitors to slash rates, and rapidly expanded digital reach across India. With unlimited voice calls and cheap data, Jio accelerated the digital transformation and brought a new wave of internet users online.
Uri Attack and Surgical Strikes
On September 18, 2016, terrorists attacked an Indian Army base in Uri, Jammu and Kashmir, killing 19 soldiers.

India responded with surgical strikes on terrorist launchpads across the LoC on September 29. This marked a strategic shift in India’s defense posture and received strong domestic backing. It heightened tensions with Pakistan and brought international attention to cross-border terrorism issues. The operation was a bold and significant military move.
PV Sindhu Wins Olympic Silver
PV Sindhu won a silver medal in badminton at the Rio Olympics in August 2016, making history as India’s youngest Olympic medalist.

She showcased resilience and skill, defeating top players and reaching the final, where she narrowly lost to Carolina Marin. Her success was celebrated nationwide and marked a high point for Indian sports. Sindhu’s achievement inspired youth across the country and highlighted the potential of Indian athletes on the global stage.
India Ratifies Paris Climate Agreement
India officially ratified the Paris Agreement on October 2, 2016, reaffirming its commitment to fighting climate change.

The date honored Mahatma Gandhi's birth anniversary, symbolizing India’s alignment with sustainable values. The agreement pledged emission reductions and expansion of renewable energy. India’s participation was crucial given its population and developmental needs. The move strengthened its position as a responsible global player.
Dipa Karmakar Makes Olympic History
Dipa Karmakar became the first Indian woman gymnast to qualify for the Olympics and reach the vault finals.

She performed the risky Produnova vault and finished fourth, just shy of a medal. Her journey from a small town in Tripura to the Olympic spotlight earned her national acclaim. Dipa’s performance shattered stereotypes and inspired young athletes, especially women, to pursue gymnastics and other non-mainstream sports.
Induction of Tejas Fighter Jet
In July 2016, India inducted its first indigenous fighter jet, Tejas, into the Indian Air Force.

Developed under the Light Combat Aircraft (LCA) program, Tejas marked a milestone in India’s defense manufacturing. It featured advanced avionics, a lightweight design, and multi-role capabilities. The induction symbolized progress toward self-reliance in defense technology and boosted India’s aerospace industry.
ISRO’s Record-Setting Reusable Launch Vehicle Test
On May 23, 2016, ISRO successfully tested India’s first Reusable Launch Vehicle-Technology Demonstrator (RLV-TD).

The winged prototype, dubbed India’s "space shuttle," was launched to demonstrate future low-cost space travel. Though unmanned, the test validated key systems like hypersonic flight and re-entry capabilities. The mission was a step toward developing reusable spacecraft and highlighted India’s innovation in space research.

Year 2016: A year of bold reforms, historic achievements, and milestones in sports, defense, and space exploration.
