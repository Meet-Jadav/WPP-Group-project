Implementation of Goods and Services Tax (GST)
On July 1, 2017, India implemented GST, replacing a complex system of indirect taxes with a unified tax structure.

The Goods and Services Tax aimed to streamline India’s taxation by combining central and state taxes into a single system. It eliminated the cascading effect of taxes and made interstate trade more efficient. Though it faced initial hurdles in implementation, GST was a significant economic reform intended to boost transparency, reduce corruption, and create a unified national market.
India Hosts FIFA U-17 World Cup
India hosted the FIFA U-17 World Cup for the first time in 2017, marking a milestone in Indian football history.

Held from October 6–28, the tournament drew international attention to India’s growing interest in football. Over 1.3 million fans attended matches across six cities, setting a new record for U-17 World Cup attendance. While India didn’t progress past the group stage, hosting the event was a key moment in promoting the sport and improving football infrastructure across the country.
Chandrayaan-2 Mission Announced
ISRO formally announced plans for Chandrayaan-2, India’s second lunar mission, aiming to explore the Moon’s south pole.

Though launched later in 2019, 2017 was when Chandrayaan-2 was publicly detailed. The mission’s goal was to soft-land a rover on the Moon and gather data on lunar terrain and mineral composition. It showcased India’s ambitions in space exploration and built upon the success of Chandrayaan-1, boosting global recognition of ISRO’s scientific capabilities.
Blue Whale Challenge Controversy
The dangerous online game known as the Blue Whale Challenge gained attention after several youth suicide cases were linked to it.

The challenge involved a series of self-harm tasks over 50 days, ending in suicide. It sparked widespread panic across schools and parents, prompting authorities to issue advisories and take down harmful content. The incident highlighted the dangers of internet challenges, mental health neglect, and the importance of digital safety awareness among young people.
India Launches 104 Satellites in One Go
ISRO created a world record on February 15, 2017, by launching 104 satellites in a single mission.

Using the PSLV-C37 rocket, ISRO deployed satellites for various countries, including the U.S., Israel, and the Netherlands. Most were nano-satellites, and the successful mission showcased India’s prowess in space technology. This achievement drew global admiration and further strengthened ISRO’s reputation as a cost-effective and reliable space agency.
Ram Nath Kovind Becomes President of India
On July 25, 2017, Ram Nath Kovind was sworn in as the 14th President of India.

Elected with over 65% of the vote, Kovind, a former Bihar governor and BJP candidate, became the second Dalit to hold the nation's highest constitutional office. His election was seen as a significant moment in Indian politics, reflecting the country’s commitment to diversity and inclusion at the highest levels of government.
Introduction of ₹200 Banknotes
The Reserve Bank of India introduced ₹200 banknotes on August 25, 2017, to improve currency circulation.

The new denomination aimed to fill the gap between ₹100 and ₹500 notes, facilitating easier transactions and reducing the burden of change. The bright yellow-colored notes featured the Sanchi Stupa on the reverse, reflecting India’s rich cultural heritage. The introduction was part of the government’s efforts to streamline the currency system post-demonetization.
Right to Privacy Declared a Fundamental Right
On August 24, 2017, the Supreme Court of India ruled that the right to privacy is a fundamental right under the Constitution.

The unanimous decision by a nine-judge bench marked a landmark moment in Indian legal history. It had widespread implications for data protection, Aadhaar, and digital rights. The ruling emphasized that privacy is intrinsic to life and liberty, strengthening the legal framework for protecting citizens in the digital age.
