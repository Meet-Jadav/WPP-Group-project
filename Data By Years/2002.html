Gujarat Riots (February 27, 2002)
Riots erupted on February 27, 2002, after the Godhra train burning incident, leading to widespread violence and unrest in Gujarat.

The violence led to over 1,000 deaths and drew national and international attention to the state's handling of the crisis.
Narmada Bachao Andolan Intensifies (March 2002)

Delhi Metro Trial Run Begins (December 24, 2002)
On December 24, 2002, the Delhi Metro began its first public trial run, signaling the beginning of modern public transportation in Delhi.

The Delhi Metro revolutionized urban transportation, improving mobility in the capital.
A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Becomes President (July 25, 2002)
On July 25, 2002, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was sworn in as the 11th President of India. His presidency was marked by his deep connection with the people.

Known as the "People's President," Kalam inspired millions of Indians, especially youth, with his vision for a developed India.
Parliament Attack Trial Verdict (December 18, 2002)
On December 18, 2002, the special court delivered its verdict on the 2001 attack on the Indian Parliament, convicting four individuals involved in the attack.

Afzal Guru, one of the accused, was sentenced to death, while others received various sentences for their roles in the attack.
Launch of National Health Mission Planning (Late 2002)
In late 2002, planning began for what would later become the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM), which was launched in 2005.

The mission aimed to improve rural healthcare infrastructure, increase access to essential healthcare services, and reduce health disparities.
