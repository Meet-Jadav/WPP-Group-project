Rewind through 2001
Gujarat Earthquake (January 26, 2001)
On January 26, 2001, a devastating 7.7 magnitude earthquake struck Bhuj, Gujarat, causing widespread destruction.

Over 20,000 lives were lost, and hundreds of thousands were injured or displaced in one of the most tragic natural disasters in India.
India-Pakistan Agra Summit (July 15–16, 2001)
The Agra Summit was a peace dialogue between India and Pakistan aimed at resolving long-standing issues between the two nations.

Despite high hopes for reconciliation, no significant agreement was reached, and the summit ended without progress.
Attack on Indian Parliament (December 13, 2001)
On December 13, 2001, terrorists attacked the Indian Parliament in New Delhi, marking a major security breach.

The attack led to the deaths of five attackers and several security personnel, pushing India to raise its alert status and increase security measures.
Launch of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (2001)
The Government of India launched the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA) in 2001 to promote universal elementary education across the country.

SSA aimed to bridge gaps in education, particularly for marginalized groups such as girls and children in rural areas.
Tehelka Corruption Scandal (March 2001)
In March 2001, the Tehelka corruption scandal broke when journalists exposed a sting operation revealing defense bribery involving senior officials.

The scandal rocked the political establishment, leading to high-profile resignations and significant political fallout.
Dhananjoy Chatterjee Verdict (2001)
In 2001, the Supreme Court of India upheld the death penalty for Dhananjoy Chatterjee, who had been convicted in a high-profile murder case.

The case sparked nationwide debates on capital punishment and the efficiency of the justice system, given its lengthy trial and appeal process.
