Mumbai Terror Attacks (26/11)
On November 26, 2008, a series of coordinated terrorist attacks took place across Mumbai, India, killing over 170 people. The attacks targeted locations like the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, CST Railway Station, and Leopold Café.

This horrific event shook the world and led to heightened security measures in India and around the globe.
Launch of the First Android Phone
In September 2008, Google and its partners released the T-Mobile G1, the first Android-powered smartphone. This marked the beginning of Android’s rise to dominance in the smartphone market.

The Android operating system soon became the most popular OS, revolutionizing the mobile phone industry.
Cricket and IPL Mania
The Indian Premier League (IPL) kicked off in April 2008, capturing the attention of millions. The league’s colorful format, cheerleaders, and star players like MS Dhoni and Shane Warne brought a fresh excitement to cricket.

The IPL became a massive success and a global phenomenon, changing the dynamics of cricket around the world.
Release of *Ghajini*
On December 25, 2008, *Ghajini*, starring Aamir Khan, was released. The film was a huge hit, becoming the first Bollywood film to cross ₹100 crore at the box office.

The movie, with its action-packed plot and Aamir Khan’s iconic "six-pack" look, became one of the biggest blockbusters of the year.
Google Chrome Browser Launched
On September 2, 2008, Google introduced the Chrome browser, which quickly gained popularity due to its speed and user-friendly interface.

Chrome became a game-changer in the browser market, eventually surpassing Internet Explorer and Mozilla Firefox in popularity.
ISRO Launches Chandrayaan-1
On October 22, 2008, ISRO launched its first lunar mission, Chandrayaan-1, which discovered water molecules on the Moon’s surface.

This achievement positioned India as the fourth country to orbit the Moon and laid the foundation for future lunar exploration missions.
Beijing Olympics 2008
From August 8–24, 2008, China hosted the Summer Olympics in Beijing, showcasing incredible technological advancements and organizational prowess. The games saw Michael Phelps break records, and Abhinav Bindra won India’s first individual Olympic gold medal in shooting.

The Beijing Olympics marked a milestone in China’s global influence and showcased its growing prominence in world sports.

Year 2008: A year of significant events in politics, technology, sports, and entertainment.
