Rewind through 2005
Irfan Pathan’s Test Hat-trick (January 29, 2005)
On January 29, 2005, Irfan Pathan took a sensational hat-trick in the first over of a Test match against Pakistan in Karachi. This was a historic moment in cricket history.

He became the first bowler to achieve this feat in the opening over of a Test match.
Manmohan Singh’s Leadership (2005)
Prime Minister Manmohan Singh continued his second year in office in 2005, focusing on economic growth and implementing key policies aimed at modernizing the Indian economy.

His government prioritized rural development, education, and social welfare schemes like the NREGA (passed in 2005).
Introduction of VAT (April 1, 2005)
On April 1, 2005, India rolled out Value Added Tax (VAT) in 21 states, replacing the complex sales tax system. This was a major step in simplifying the tax structure across the country.

It aimed to streamline indirect taxation and improve compliance.
RTI Act Enacted (June 15, 2005)
On June 15, 2005, The RTI Act was passed by the Indian Parliament, and it came into effect in October 2005, empowering citizens to seek information from public authorities.

The Act was a significant step towards enhancing transparency and accountability in governance.
London Bombings (July 7, 2005)
On July 7, 2005, four coordinated suicide bomb attacks struck London’s public transport system during rush hour, resulting in one of the deadliest terrorist attacks in the city.

The bombings resulted in 52 civilian deaths and over 700 injuries, and they led to significant changes in security policies.
YouTube Launched (2005)
YouTube was founded in 2005 by Chad Hurley, Steve Chen, and Jawed Karim, marking the beginning of a new era for internet culture, video sharing, and digital content creation.

It would go on to become the largest video-sharing platform globally, revolutionizing entertainment and media consumption.
